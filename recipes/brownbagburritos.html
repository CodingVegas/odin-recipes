Brown Bag Burritos

Brown bag burritos are easy to freeze

What I like best about this recipe is that, not only is it filling, but it’s also fast. You can have a piping hot burrito in less than two minutes — straight from the freezer. It’s perfect for those times when my husband wants a late-night snack or an easy weeknight dinner.

These are little too much on the spicy side for my liking (keep in mind that I’m also the girl who likes extra mild salsa!), but my husband loves these. In fact, he was so excited I was making them again this week.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds ground beef
  • 2 cans refried beans
  • 2 cans enchilada sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/3 cup diced onion
  • 1 Tablespoon chili powder
  • 1 Tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 3 cups shredded mexican cheese blend
  • 20 burrito-sized flour tortillas

Steps

  1. Brown ground beef with onions.
  2. Drain beef and add all remaining ingredients except tortillas and cheese.
  3. Bring mixture to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 20-30 minutes.
  4. Stir in cheese untill melted.
  5. Spoon 1/4-cup of the meat mixture onto a tortilla. Fold sides in and roll up tightly.

To freeze: Put burritos in two airtight ziptop freezer bags and store in the freezer for 2-3 months.

To serve: Pull out desired number of burritos. Heat a single burrito in the microwave for about a minute or leave the foil on and heat one or more burritos in a preheated 350-degree oven for 30 minutes or until heated through.
